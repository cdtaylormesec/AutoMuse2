Mr. Collins is a different man around his equals than he is around less prosperous people. He is always proud, but around the rich he is also liberal-minded, just, sincere, rational, honourable, and perhaps agreeable. He assured Mrs. Phillips that it was not of the least importance that he had lost every point, that he considered the money as a mere trifle, and begged that she would not make herself uneasy.
