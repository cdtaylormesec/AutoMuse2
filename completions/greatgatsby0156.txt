Then the same half-knowing, half-bewildered look came back into his faded eyes.  “Look in the drawer there,” he said, pointing at the desk.  “Which drawer?”  “That drawer—that one.”  Michaelis opened the drawer nearest his hand. There was nothing in it but a small, expensive dog-leash, made of leather and braided silver. It was apparently new.  “This?” he inquired, holding it up.  Wilson stared and nodded.  “I found it yesterday afternoon.
