No       principle of either would be violated by my marriage with Mr.       Darcy. And with regard to the resentment of his family, or the       indignation of the world, if the former _were_ excited by his       marrying me, it would not give me one moment’s concern—and the       world in general would have too much sense to join in the scorn.”        “And this is your real opinion! This is your final resolve! Very       well. I shall now know how to act. Do not imagine, Miss Bennet,       that your ambition will ever be gratified. I came to try you. I       hoped to find you reasonable; but, depend upon it, I will carry       my point.”        In this manner Lady Catherine talked on, till they were at the       door of the carriage, when, turning hastily round, she added, “I       take no leave of you, Miss Bennet. I send no compliments to your       mother. You deserve no such attention. I am most seriously       displeased.”        Elizabeth made no answer; and without attempting to persuade her       ladyship to return into the house, walked quietly into it       herself.
