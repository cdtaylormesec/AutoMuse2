Society has claims on us all; and I profess myself       one of those who consider intervals of recreation and amusement       as desirable for everybody.”        Elizabeth’s spirits were so high on this occasion, that though       she did not often speak unnecessarily to Mr. Collins, she could       not help asking him whether he intended to accept Mr. Bingley’s       invitation, and if he did, whether he would think it proper to       join in the evening’s amusement; and she was rather surprised to       find that he entertained no scruple whatever on that head, and       was very far from dreading a rebuke either from the Archbishop,       or Lady Catherine de Bourgh, by venturing to dance.        “I am by no means of the opinion, I assure you,” said he, “that a       ball of this kind, given by a young man of character, to       respectable people, can have any evil tendency; and I am so far       from objecting to dancing myself, that I shall hope to be       honoured with the hands of all my fair cousins in the course of       the evening; and I take this opportunity of soliciting yours,       Miss Elizabeth, for the two first dances especially, a preference       which I trust my cousin Jane will attribute to the right cause,       and not to any disrespect for her.”        Elizabeth felt herself completely taken in.
