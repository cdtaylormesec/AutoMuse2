The palings of Rosings Park was their boundary       on one side. Elizabeth smiled at the recollection of all that she       had heard of its inhabitants.        At length the Parsonage was discernible. The garden sloping to       the road, the house standing in it, the green pales, and the       laurel hedge, everything declared they were arriving. Mr. Collins       and Charlotte appeared at the door, and the carriage stopped at       the small gate which led by a short gravel walk to the house,       amidst the nods and smiles of the whole party. In a moment they       were all out of the chaise, rejoicing at the sight of each other.
