But I believe your opinion       of him would in general astonish—and perhaps you would not       express it quite so strongly anywhere else. Here you are in your       own family.”        “Upon my word, I say no more _here_ than I might say in any house       in the neighbourhood, except Netherfield. He is not at all liked       in Hertfordshire. Everybody is disgusted with his pride. You will       not find him more favourably spoken of by anyone.”        “I cannot pretend to be sorry,” said Wickham, after a short       interruption, “that he or that any man should not be estimated       beyond their deserts; but with _him_ I believe it does not often       happen. The world is blinded by his fortune and consequence, or       frightened by his high and imposing manners, and sees him only as       he chooses to be seen.”        “I should take him, even on _my_ slight acquaintance, to be an       ill-tempered man.” Wickham only shook his head.        “I wonder,” said he, at the next opportunity of speaking,       “whether he is likely to be in this country much longer.”        “I do not at all know; but I _heard_ nothing of his going away       when I was at Netherfield.
