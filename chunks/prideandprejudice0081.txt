There is no       knowing how estates will go when once they come to be entailed.”        “I am very sensible, madam, of the hardship to my fair cousins,       and could say much on the subject, but that I am cautious of       appearing forward and precipitate. But I can assure the young       ladies that I come prepared to admire them. At present I will not       say more; but, perhaps, when we are better acquainted—”        He was interrupted by a summons to dinner; and the girls smiled       on each other. They were not the only objects of Mr. Collins’s       admiration. The hall, the dining-room, and all its furniture,       were examined and praised; and his commendation of everything       would have touched Mrs. Bennet’s heart, but for the mortifying       supposition of his viewing it all as his own future property. The       dinner too in its turn was highly admired; and he begged to know       to which of his fair cousins the excellency of its cooking was       owing.
