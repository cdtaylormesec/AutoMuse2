He took leave of his relations at       Longbourn with as much solemnity as before; wished his fair       cousins health and happiness again, and promised their father       another letter of thanks.        On the following Monday, Mrs. Bennet had the pleasure of       receiving her brother and his wife, who came as usual to spend       the Christmas at Longbourn. Mr. Gardiner was a sensible,       gentlemanlike man, greatly superior to his sister, as well by       nature as education. The Netherfield ladies would have had       difficulty in believing that a man who lived by trade, and within       view of his own warehouses, could have been so well-bred and       agreeable. Mrs. Gardiner, who was several years younger than Mrs.       Bennet and Mrs. Phillips, was an amiable, intelligent, elegant       woman, and a great favourite with all her Longbourn nieces.       Between the two eldest and herself especially, there subsisted a       particular regard. They had frequently been staying with her in       town.
