She was not       rendered formidable by silence; but whatever she said was spoken       in so authoritative a tone, as marked her self-importance, and       brought Mr. Wickham immediately to Elizabeth’s mind; and from the       observation of the day altogether, she believed Lady Catherine to       be exactly what he represented.        When, after examining the mother, in whose countenance and       deportment she soon found some resemblance of Mr. Darcy, she       turned her eyes on the daughter, she could almost have joined in       Maria’s astonishment at her being so thin and so small. There was       neither in figure nor face any likeness between the ladies. Miss       de Bourgh was pale and sickly; her features, though not plain,       were insignificant; and she spoke very little, except in a low       voice, to Mrs. Jenkinson, in whose appearance there was nothing       remarkable, and who was entirely engaged in listening to what she       said, and placing a screen in the proper direction before her       eyes.        After sitting a few minutes, they were all sent to one of the       windows to admire the view, Mr. Collins attending them to point       out its beauties, and Lady Catherine kindly informing them that       it was much better worth looking at in the summer.
