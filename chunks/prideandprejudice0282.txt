Chapter 39        It was the second week in May, in which the three young ladies       set out together from Gracechurch Street for the town of ——, in       Hertfordshire; and, as they drew near the appointed inn where Mr.       Bennet’s carriage was to meet them, they quickly perceived, in       token of the coachman’s punctuality, both Kitty and Lydia looking       out of a dining-room up stairs. These two girls had been above an       hour in the place, happily employed in visiting an opposite       milliner, watching the sentinel on guard, and dressing a salad       and cucumber.        After welcoming their sisters, they triumphantly displayed a       table set out with such cold meat as an inn larder usually       affords, exclaiming, “Is not this nice?
