I do think Mrs. Long       is as good a creature as ever lived—and her nieces are very       pretty behaved girls, and not at all handsome: I like them       prodigiously.”        Mrs. Bennet, in short, was in very great spirits; she had seen       enough of Bingley’s behaviour to Jane, to be convinced that she       would get him at last; and her expectations of advantage to her       family, when in a happy humour, were so far beyond reason, that       she was quite disappointed at not seeing him there again the next       day, to make his proposals.        “It has been a very agreeable day,” said Miss Bennet to       Elizabeth. “The party seemed so well selected, so suitable one       with the other.
