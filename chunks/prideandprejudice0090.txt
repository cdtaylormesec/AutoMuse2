The officer was the very Mr. Denny concerning whose return       from London Lydia came to enquire, and he bowed as they passed.       All were struck with the stranger’s air, all wondered who he       could be; and Kitty and Lydia, determined if possible to find       out, led the way across the street, under pretense of wanting       something in an opposite shop, and fortunately had just gained       the pavement when the two gentlemen, turning back, had reached       the same spot. Mr. Denny addressed them directly, and entreated       permission to introduce his friend, Mr. Wickham, who had returned       with him the day before from town, and he was happy to say had       accepted a commission in their corps. This was exactly as it       should be; for the young man wanted only regimentals to make him       completely charming. His appearance was greatly in his favour; he       had all the best part of beauty, a fine countenance, a good       figure, and very pleasing address.
