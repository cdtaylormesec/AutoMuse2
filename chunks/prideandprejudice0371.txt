It is not quite a week since they left Brighton. In a       few days more we may gain some news of them; and till we know       that they are not married, and have no design of marrying, do not       let us give the matter over as lost. As soon as I get to town I       shall go to my brother, and make him come home with me to       Gracechurch Street; and then we may consult together as to what       is to be done.”        “Oh! my dear brother,” replied Mrs. Bennet, “that is exactly what       I could most wish for. And now do, when you get to town, find       them out, wherever they may be; and if they are not married       already, _make_ them marry.
