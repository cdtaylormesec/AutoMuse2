“I wish you joy. If you love Mr. Darcy half as well as I do my       dear Wickham, you must be very happy. It is a great comfort to       have you so rich, and when you have nothing else to do, I hope       you will think of us. I am sure Wickham would like a place at       court very much, and I do not think we shall have quite money       enough to live upon without some help. Any place would do, of       about three or four hundred a year; but however, do not speak to       Mr. Darcy about it, if you had rather not.        “Yours, etc.”        As it happened that Elizabeth had much rather not, she       endeavoured in her answer to put an end to every entreaty and       expectation of the kind. Such relief, however, as it was in her       power to afford, by the practice of what might be called economy       in her own private expences, she frequently sent them. It had       always been evident to her that such an income as theirs, under       the direction of two persons so extravagant in their wants, and       heedless of the future, must be very insufficient to their       support; and whenever they changed their quarters, either Jane or       herself were sure of being applied to for some little assistance       towards discharging their bills. Their manner of living, even       when the restoration of peace dismissed them to a home, was       unsettled in the extreme.
