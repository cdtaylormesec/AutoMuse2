Barsad proposed to the rest, “Let her embrace him then; it is but a moment.” It was silently acquiesced in, and they passed her over the seats in the hall to a raised place, where he, by leaning over the dock, could fold her in his arms.  “Farewell, dear darling of my soul. My parting blessing on my love. We shall meet again, where the weary are at rest!”  They were her husband’s words, as he held her to his bosom.  “I can bear it, dear Charles. I am supported from above: don’t suffer for me. A parting blessing for our child.”  “I send it to her by you. I kiss her by you. I say farewell to her by you.”  “My husband. No! A moment!” He was tearing himself apart from her. “We shall not be separated long. I feel that this will break my heart by-and-bye; but I will do my duty while I can, and when I leave her, God will raise up friends for her, as He did for me.”  Her father had followed her, and would have fallen on his knees to both of them, but that Darnay put out a hand and seized him, crying:  “No, no! What have you done, what have you done, that you should kneel to us! We know now, what a struggle you made of old. We know, now what you underwent when you suspected my descent, and when you knew it. We know now, the natural antipathy you strove against, and conquered, for her dear sake. We thank you with all our hearts, and all our love and duty.
