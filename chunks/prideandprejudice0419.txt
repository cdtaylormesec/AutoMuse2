Your father was gone, your uncle at       home, and, as I said before, they had a great deal of talk       together.        “They met again on Sunday, and then _I_ saw him too. It was not       all settled before Monday: as soon as it was, the express was       sent off to Longbourn. But our visitor was very obstinate. I       fancy, Lizzy, that obstinacy is the real defect of his character,       after all. He has been accused of many faults at different times,       but _this_ is the true one. Nothing was to be done that he did       not do himself; though I am sure (and I do not speak it to be       thanked, therefore say nothing about it), your uncle would most       readily have settled the whole.        “They battled it together for a long time, which was more than       either the gentleman or lady concerned in it deserved. But at       last your uncle was forced to yield, and instead of being allowed       to be of use to his niece, was forced to put up with only having       the probable credit of it, which went sorely against the grain;       and I really believe your letter this morning gave him great       pleasure, because it required an explanation that would rob him       of his borrowed feathers, and give the praise where it was due.
