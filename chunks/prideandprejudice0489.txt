He is rich,       to be sure, and you may have more fine clothes and fine carriages       than Jane. But will they make you happy?”        “Have you any other objection,” said Elizabeth, “than your belief       of my indifference?”        “None at all. We all know him to be a proud, unpleasant sort of       man; but this would be nothing if you really liked him.”        “I do, I do like him,” she replied, with tears in her eyes, “I       love him. Indeed he has no improper pride. He is perfectly       amiable. You do not know what he really is; then pray do not pain       me by speaking of him in such terms.”        “Lizzy,” said her father, “I have given him my consent. He is the       kind of man, indeed, to whom I should never dare refuse anything,       which he condescended to ask. I now give it to _you_, if you are       resolved on having him. But let me advise you to think better of       it. I know your disposition, Lizzy.
