In a       doleful voice Mrs. Bennet began the projected conversation: “Oh!       Mr. Collins!”        “My dear madam,” replied he, “let us be for ever silent on this       point. Far be it from me,” he presently continued, in a voice       that marked his displeasure, “to resent the behaviour of your       daughter. Resignation to inevitable evils is the duty of us all;       the peculiar duty of a young man who has been so fortunate as I       have been in early preferment; and I trust I am resigned. Perhaps       not the less so from feeling a doubt of my positive happiness had       my fair cousin honoured me with her hand; for I have often       observed that resignation is never so perfect as when the       blessing denied begins to lose somewhat of its value in our       estimation.
