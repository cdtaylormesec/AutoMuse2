A promise of secrecy was of       course very dutifully given, but it could not be kept without       difficulty; for the curiosity excited by his long absence burst       forth in such very direct questions on his return as required       some ingenuity to evade, and he was at the same time exercising       great self-denial, for he was longing to publish his prosperous       love.        As he was to begin his journey too early on the morrow to see any       of the family, the ceremony of leave-taking was performed when       the ladies moved for the night; and Mrs. Bennet, with great       politeness and cordiality, said how happy they should be to see       him at Longbourn again, whenever his engagements might allow him       to visit them.        “My dear madam,” he replied, “this invitation is particularly       gratifying, because it is what I have been hoping to receive; and       you may be very certain that I shall avail myself of it as soon       as possible.”        They were all astonished; and Mr. Bennet, who could by no means       wish for so speedy a return, immediately said:        “But is there not danger of Lady Catherine’s disapprobation here,       my good sir?
