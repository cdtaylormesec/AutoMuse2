You are a turnkey at the Conciergerie?”  “I tell you once for all, there is no such thing as an escape possible,”  said the spy, firmly.  “Why need you tell me what I have not asked? You are a turnkey at the Conciergerie?”  “I am sometimes.”  “You can be when you choose?”  “I can pass in and out when I choose.”  Sydney Carton filled another glass with brandy, poured it slowly out upon the hearth, and watched it as it dropped. It being all spent, he said, rising:  “So far, we have spoken before these two, because it was as well that the merits of the cards should not rest solely between you and me. Come into the dark room here, and let us have one final word alone.”     CHAPTER IX.
