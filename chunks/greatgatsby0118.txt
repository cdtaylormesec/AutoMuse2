Let’s all go to town!”  Her voice struggled on through the heat, beating against it, moulding its senselessness into forms.  “I’ve heard of making a garage out of a stable,” Tom was saying to Gatsby, “but I’m the first man who ever made a stable out of a garage.”  “Who wants to go to town?” demanded Daisy insistently. Gatsby’s eyes floated toward her. “Ah,” she cried, “you look so cool.”  Their eyes met, and they stared together at each other, alone in space. With an effort she glanced down at the table.  “You always look so cool,” she repeated.  She had told him that she loved him, and Tom Buchanan saw. He was astounded. His mouth opened a little, and he looked at Gatsby, and then back at Daisy as if he had just recognized her as someone he knew a long time ago.  “You resemble the advertisement of the man,” she went on innocently. “You know the advertisement of the man—”  “All right,” broke in Tom quickly, “I’m perfectly willing to go to town.
