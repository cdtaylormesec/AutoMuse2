In the absence of Jane, he always attached himself to Elizabeth,       for the pleasure of talking of her; and when Bingley was gone,       Jane constantly sought the same means of relief.        “He has made me so happy,” said she, one evening, “by telling me       that he was totally ignorant of my being in town last spring! I       had not believed it possible.”        “I suspected as much,” replied Elizabeth. “But how did he account       for it?”        “It must have been his sister’s doing. They were certainly no       friends to his acquaintance with me, which I cannot wonder at,       since he might have chosen so much more advantageously in many       respects. But when they see, as I trust they will, that their       brother is happy with me, they will learn to be contented, and we       shall be on good terms again; though we can never be what we once       were to each other.”        “That is the most unforgiving speech,” said Elizabeth, “that I       ever heard you utter.
